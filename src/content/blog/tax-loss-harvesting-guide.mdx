## What is Tax-Loss Harvesting?

If you've ever cringed at your year-end tax bill, you're not alone. Many American investors struggle with the complexity of investment taxes and the impact they have on their returns. Enter **tax-loss harvesting**—a strategy that can help you reduce those taxes by offsetting capital gains with capital losses.

In simple terms, tax-loss harvesting involves selling investments that have decreased in value to realize a loss, which can then be used to offset taxable gains from other investments. This strategy is particularly useful for investors with multiple US brokerage accounts and a diverse portfolio.

## How Tax-Loss Harvesting Reduces Taxes

The primary goal of tax-loss harvesting is to **reduce investment taxes** by offsetting gains with losses. Let's break it down with an example:

Imagine you sold a stock for a $10,000 gain this year. You also have another stock that's down by $5,000. By selling the underperforming stock, you can "harvest" that $5,000 loss. Now, instead of paying taxes on the full $10,000 gain, you'll only pay taxes on a $5,000 net gain.

### Benefits for US Investors

- **Offset Capital Gains:** Use losses to offset gains in the same year, effectively lowering your taxable income.
- **Carry Forward Losses:** If your losses exceed your gains, you can carry them forward to future tax years, providing ongoing tax benefits.
- **Strategic Rebalancing:** Tax-loss harvesting gives you an opportunity to rebalance your portfolio without incurring tax penalties.

## Wash Sale Rules Explained

One crucial aspect of tax-loss harvesting is understanding the **wash sale rules**. These rules are designed to prevent investors from selling a security at a loss and then quickly repurchasing it to claim the tax benefits.

### What is a Wash Sale?

A wash sale occurs when you sell a security at a loss and buy the same or a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale. If a wash sale is triggered, the IRS disallows the loss, and it cannot be used to offset gains.

### Avoiding the Wash Sale Trap

- **Keep Track of Replacements:** When selling a stock, avoid buying it back within the 30-day window.
- **Use Different Securities:** Consider similar but not identical securities if you wish to maintain market exposure.
- **Mind Your 401(k) and IRA:** Wash sale rules apply across all US accounts, including 401(k), IRA, and Roth IRA.

## When to Harvest Losses

Timing is crucial when it comes to tax-loss harvesting. Here are some tips to help you decide when to harvest tax losses:

- **Year-End Planning:** Many investors review their portfolios at year-end to identify potential losses and offset gains realized throughout the year.
- **Market Volatility:** Periods of market downturns can present opportunities to realize losses.
- **Quarterly Reviews:** Regularly review your accounts to identify underperforming investments.
