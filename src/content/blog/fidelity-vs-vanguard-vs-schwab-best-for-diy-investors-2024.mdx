# Fidelity vs Vanguard vs Schwab: Which Is Best for DIY Investors in 2024?

Are you a DIY investor in the UK feeling overwhelmed by the fragmented views of your portfolio across multiple platforms? You're not alone. With so many options like Fidelity, Vanguard, and Schwab, choosing the best brokerage can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack. Let's break down these three heavyweights to help you make an informed decision for your investment journey in 2024.

## Overview of Each Broker

### Fidelity
Fidelity is a well-regarded name in the investment world, known for its robust research tools and extensive range of investment options. For UK investors, Fidelity offers a Stocks and Shares ISA and SIPP, making it a flexible option for those looking to manage their UK pension and other investments.

### Vanguard
Vanguard is synonymous with index funds, and for good reason. It's the best brokerage for index funds due to its low-cost philosophy and wide array of passive investment options. Vanguard's Stocks and Shares ISA and SIPP are particularly appealing to cost-conscious investors focused on long-term growth.

### Schwab
Schwab, while more US-centric, offers competitive services to UK investors. Its range of investment options and low-cost structure make it an attractive choice for those looking to diversify into international markets, including access to UK-specific investments like the FTSE 100.

## Fee Comparison

When it comes to fees, every penny saved is a penny earned, especially for UK investors managing ISAs or SIPPs. Here's a quick breakdown:

- **Fidelity**: Charges a platform fee of 0.35% per annum for a Stocks and Shares ISA and SIPP, with no additional fees on regular fund purchases.
- **Vanguard**: Known for its low-cost structure, Vanguard charges a 0.15% annual account fee with a cap of £375 for investments in a Stocks and Shares ISA.
- **Schwab**: Offers zero commission on stock trades in the US, but UK investors should be mindful of potential currency conversion fees.

## Fund Selection

A diverse fund selection is crucial for building a resilient portfolio:

- **Fidelity**: Offers over 3,000 funds, providing access to a broad spectrum of asset classes and geographies.
- **Vanguard**: Specializes in index funds and ETFs, with a strong focus on low-cost options. They offer a solid selection of Vanguard FTSE 100 ETFs for UK investors.
- **Schwab**: Provides access to a wide array of mutual funds, ETFs, and stocks, including international markets.

## Trading Platforms

The user experience can make or break your investment process:

- **Fidelity**: Offers a user-friendly platform with powerful research tools and mobile app functionality.
- **Vanguard**: While its platform is straightforward and easy to use, it may lack some of the advanced features seen with competitors.
- **Schwab**: Known for a robust trading platform with comprehensive research tools and a highly rated mobile app.

## Customer Service

Having reliable support can ease many frustrations:

- **Fidelity**: Offers 24/7 customer support, with a reputation for being responsive and helpful.
- **Vanguard**: Provides customer service during business hours with a focus on helping investors make informed decisions.
- **Schwab**: Known for excellent customer service, available 24/7, catering to a global clientele.

## Who Should Choose Which?

- **Choose Fidelity** if you value extensive research tools and a wide selection of funds for your Stocks and Shares ISA or SIPP.
- **Choose Vanguard** if low-cost index funds are your priority and you're looking to build a simple, cost-effective portfolio.
- **Choose Schwab** if you're interested in accessing international markets and appreciate a sophisticated trading platform.
